# Product

## Register

product

## Users

VetTrack is used by veterinary clinicians, technicians, and shift leads inside busy clinic wards. They are often moving, gloved, one-handed, and under time pressure while scanning equipment, checking custody, resolving status issues, and handing off shift responsibilities.

## Product Purpose

VetTrack is an offline-first native mobile app for veterinary equipment tracking. It replaces the current Capacitor WebView with a faster, clearer Expo experience for NFC scanning, equipment status, rooms, alerts, shift handoff, and honest offline sync. Success means clinical staff can find, scan, return, and escalate equipment with less friction while safety-critical Code Blue behavior remains unmistakable and never falsely queued offline.

## Brand Personality

Clinical, calm, decisive. The product should feel like the same VetTrack brand leveled up: deep forest green, ivory surfaces, clear status language, dense but readable information, and motion that explains system state.

## Anti-references

Do not make the native app feel like a squeezed desktop dashboard, a generic Expo starter app, a blue SaaS template, or a decorative healthcare concept mockup. Avoid web-table baggage, hardcoded English/Hebrew copy, over-spaced cards that hide operational detail, spinner-only loading, hidden emergency states, fake offline promises for emergency actions, and any new ER/patient/hospitalization/admin surface outside the June 2026 scope cut.

## Design Principles

1. Preserve the brand, improve the ergonomics.
2. Make equipment triage scannable before it is pretty.
3. Put primary actions in thumb reach and make scan the signature gesture.
4. Treat offline, conflict, and emergency states as first-class product states.
5. Design Hebrew and RTL as the primary experience, with English/LTR as a mirrored secondary case.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use 44-48px minimum tap targets, high-contrast text and status treatments, safe-area-aware layouts, reduced-motion alternatives, and color-plus-text status indicators. Designs must support Hebrew RTL defaults, English LTR mirroring, screen-reader labels, gloved one-handed use, and clear non-color cues for critical, warning, offline, and sync states.
